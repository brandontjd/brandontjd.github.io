Intro

I'm a Year 2 student at Singapore Management University, pursuing a double degree in Business Management and Information Systems. My specialisations are in Quantitative Finance and Business Analytics.

I've had the opportunity to intern at fast growing consumer internet companies such as Foodpanda and Klook. There, I've gained insights into the applications of Machine Learning to boost profit margins and spearheaded successful projects myself. Now, I've decided to pursue a career in Quantitative Finance, specifically in applying Machine Learning techniques to manage risk and uncover alpha factors.

"Backtesting can never be a true positive, it can only flag out false positives." - Marcos Lopez.

All too often, financial research papers have been perverted by p-hacking and false backtesting. Hence, on this journey that I'm on, I'm determined to use Machine Learning responsibly. It will not only be used to generate alpha but also as a tool to uncover financial theorems that can provide a true positive, one that is not backed solely by backtesting.

Work

My first project involved investigating the presence of the "Post Earnings Announcement Drift" and "Accrual" anomalies in the financial markets. Concretely, I found a viable trading strategy that garnered 37% returns over the course of 3 years (notably, S&P500 still performs better!). My work can be found here.

My second project utilised the development of eigen-portfolios to decompose a basket of stocks (in my case the Dow Jones Index) into their underlying factors and trading on the best performing factor. The results found that one could significantly out perform the DJI by simply adjusting the weights of each stock and having an appropriate stop loss in place. The findings can be found here.

In previous internships, I've also completed key projects that raised our profit margins significantly.

  1. Utilising clustering algorithms to segment customer groups to conduct A/B testing
  2. Scraping competitor inventories and reviews to understand what consumers want
  3. Natural Language Model to map competitor products to our own products
  4. Set up foodpanda's catering business by finding and negotitating with vendors (non-coding)
For the relevant works, due to past employment agreements, I will not be able to disclose the code.

Upcoming works in the pipeline:

  1. A hierarchical approach to predicting HDB resale flat prices
  2. The applications of Marchenko-Pastur, as follow up from eigen-decomposition
  3. Can spotify predict stock prices?
